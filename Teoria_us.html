COUSIN COST

They are direct materials and direct labor. These costs are directly related to production. For example, the cost of labor (doctors, nurses, etc.) and direct materials (examination and treatment) Amount of direct material and direct labor involved in the manufacture of a product.



AMOUNT OF DIRECT MATERIAL AND OF THE HAND OF OBRADIRECTA THAT INTERVENE IN THE MANUFACTURE OF A PRODUCT.


To determine the cost incurred by a company to manufacture a product, a record and control must be kept of all the elements that make up that cost, including the raw material needed in the preparation of your product. Now, to process the raw material, it requires personnel that directly manipulate and process the raw material; It is what is known as direct labor. Within direct labor, as is obvious, personnel expenses that do not participate directly in the process of transforming the raw material, such as secretaries, salespersons, managers, etc., are not included. Within the personnel expenses that make up the direct labor, all the concepts paid to them are included, such as salaries, social benefits, social security, parafiscal contributions, endowment,

PRODUCTION COST

Production costs are monetary estimates of all expenses that have been made within the company, for the development of a good. These expenses include everything related to labor, costs of materials, as well as all indirect costs that in some way contribute to the manufacture of a good.


A company to achieve its goals must obtain from its environment the elements it needs for the production of a good or service, among which are: labor, raw materials, machinery, capital, etc.



The production costs are divided into:


Fixed costs:they are the permanent costs of the company, so their disbursement is not subject to the level of production, that is, if the company produces or not, they must be paid equally. For example: payments for rent, salaries and salaries, utility expenses (electricity, water, telephone, etc.)


Variable costs:those that can be increased or decreased, depending on the degree of production. For example: the raw material, if the sales of a product increase, then you will need much more raw material to make it, or on the contrary, if the sales of a product decrease, you will not need much raw material. The same happens with the containers, since their quantity will obey the quantity of goods manufactured.



Total cost: It is the sum of the fixed and variable costs.


Marginal cost: represents the variation rate of the total cost, before the increase of one unit produced. For example: if the total cost to produce 50 products is 100 pesos and the total cost to manufacture 51 products is 115 pesos, that is, the marginal cost is 15 pesos.

INDIRECT COSTS

Indirect costs are those that are not directly attributable to the production of a particular product or service. Among them, some authors distinguish between so-called semidirects, when in spite of not being able to be attributed to a specific product or service, they can be imputed to a specific cost center or department. And, the indirect ones, which would then be those that it is not possible to impute to a specific product or service, or to any specific cost center or department.


In addition, it is usual to distinguish between indirect costs of production, which are those costs not directly involved in production but necessary for it, such as indirect labor. And, the general indirect costs, which, in turn, are usually divided into several groups. such as indirect business costs, indirect costs of administration, and indirect costs of a financial nature.



Examples of indirect costs:


  • Rentals
  • Taxes
  • Internet and telephone expenses
  • Amortization
  • Fuels



    • DISTRIBUTION COSTS

    Distribution costs are defined as the monetary value that results from the consumption or wear of a set of productive factors. They refer to the expenditures (expenses and investments) that companies make to distribute their products in the market.


    Distribution costs are made up of marketing and financing expenses. Include the costs of packaging, special packaging, storage, transfer of goods, those generated by the payment of personnel responsible for selling the product, administration and waste.



    Fundamental aspects of distribution:


    Create the demand. The creation of the demand consists in awakening the interest of the consumer through advertising, in order to obtain potential buyers.


    Get the order. Obtaining the order arises when you already have a buyer. That is, when the client (supermarkets, companies, stores, among others) decides to buy a certain amount of the product offered.


    The handling and delivery of the product. It refers to the way in which the merchandise is packed, stored and transported, until it reaches the place specified by the buyer.


    Sales control. This aspect covers every detail in economic matters. It is considered as the monitoring carried out from the moment in which they decide to capture the customers until the process of delivery of the product.



    How distribution costs are identified


  • Kind of product.
  • Areas where each product will be sold.
  • Types of customers or consumers.
  • Elements of the sale.
  • Group the distribution costs according to the function of the sale.
  • Centralize them in a single delivery concept.


    • TOTAL COST OF OPERATION

    The total cost of operation is the result of adding the Production cost and the Distribution cost

    UNIT COST OF PRODUCTION

    The unit cost is the monetary value of a specific product, that is: how much it costs us to produce and distribute accounting for all costs.


    It is the cost of producing a unit of product or service, based on averages and taking into account the costs involved in manufacturing (Fixed and variable costs).


    Represents the sum of the unit costs of the inputs (Example: direct materials, direct labor, and indirect manufacturing costs).


    The unit cost is determined by dividing the total cost by the amount of projected production. The necessary information is the following:



    Projected costs of:


  • Direct material
  • Direct labor
  • Manufacturing expenses
